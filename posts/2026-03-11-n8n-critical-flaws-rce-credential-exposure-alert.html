n8n Critical Flaws: RCE & Credential Exposure Alert

By AI Security & Compliance Desk March 11, 2026

Source: The Hacker News

Imagine your meticulously crafted automation workflows – the very engine of your operational efficiency – suddenly becoming a backdoor for attackers. This isn't a dystopian IT nightmare; it's a stark reality for organizations utilizing the n8n workflow automation platform. Cybersecurity researchers have recently unveiled details of two critical, now-patched security flaws that could turn your automated processes into vectors for remote code execution and the exposure of highly sensitive credentials. For IT professionals, security teams, and compliance officers, this news demands immediate attention and a thorough review of your security posture.

As reported by The Hacker News on March 11, 2026, these vulnerabilities in n8n – an open-source tool designed to connect apps and automate workflows – carry severe implications. While n8n's power lies in its ability to streamline complex tasks, these newly disclosed flaws highlight the inherent risks when such powerful tools are compromised.

Understanding the Critical Vulnerabilities

Two specific Common Vulnerabilities and Exposures (CVEs) stand out, each presenting a grave threat:

  • CVE-2026-27577 (CVSS score: 9.4) – Expression Sandbox Escape leading to Remote Code Execution (RCE): This vulnerability is particularly insidious. n8n utilizes an 'expression sandbox' to safely execute custom code within workflows. An escape from this sandbox means an attacker could bypass these security controls, execute arbitrary commands on the underlying server, and potentially gain full control of the system hosting n8n. The implications range from data theft and system defacement to the deployment of ransomware or persistent backdoors.
  • CVE-2026-27493 (CVSS score: 9.5) – Unauthenticated Access leading to Stored Credential Exposure: With an even higher CVSS score, this flaw is a red flag for any security team. It allows unauthenticated access, meaning an attacker doesn't need legitimate credentials to exploit it. The critical danger here is the exposure of stored credentials – API keys, database passwords, OAuth tokens, and other secrets that n8n uses to connect to various services. Access to these credentials could grant attackers unfettered access to a wide array of connected systems, escalating a localized compromise into a full-scale enterprise breach.

The Real-World Impact on Your Organization

For organizations leveraging n8n, the potential fallout from these vulnerabilities is significant. An RCE flaw could lead to complete system compromise, allowing attackers to manipulate or destroy data, disrupt critical operations, and establish a persistent presence within your network. The exposure of stored credentials, on the other hand, is a direct pathway to lateral movement across your IT infrastructure, potentially compromising every system and service connected through n8n. This could result in:

  • Massive Data Breaches: Sensitive customer data, intellectual property, and internal records could be exfiltrated.
  • Operational Paralysis: Critical workflows could be halted, corrupted, or weaponized.
  • Compliance Violations: Breaches involving sensitive data (e.g., PII, PHI) will trigger stringent reporting requirements and potentially hefty fines under regulations like GDPR, HIPAA, and SOC 2.
  • Reputational Damage: A public data breach can severely erode customer trust and brand value.

Immediate Actions and Proactive Security Measures

Given the severity, immediate action is paramount:

  1. Patch Immediately: Ensure all n8n instances are updated to the latest patched versions as soon as possible. This is the most critical first step.
  2. Audit for Compromise: Conduct a thorough forensic analysis of your n8n instances and connected systems for any signs of unauthorized access or activity. Look for unusual logs, modified files, or new user accounts.
  3. Review Stored Credentials: Rotate all credentials stored within n8n, especially if there's any suspicion of compromise. Implement stringent access controls and least privilege principles.
  4. Network Segmentation: Isolate your n8n deployment within your network to minimize potential lateral movement in case of a breach.
  5. Monitor for Anomalies: Enhance monitoring for unusual activity originating from your n8n servers or related accounts.
